# Project Handoff: Amanah-e-Kisa / Amanah Quran

## Locked Project Identity

**Project identity:** Amanah-e-Kisa
**Urdu:** امانتِ کِساء

**Public app name:** Amanah Quran
**Urdu:** امانہ قرآن

**Preferred domain:** amanahquran.org

**Project nature:** Charity / Sadaqah Jariyah project
**Commercial model:** Completely free, no ads, no in-app purchases, no monetization pressure.

## Core Vision

Build a highly trustworthy, beautiful, offline-first Android Quran application that is completely free, ad-free, privacy-respecting, and accessible for all Muslims.

The app should be inspired by the concept of **Amanah**: sacred trust, responsibility, authenticity, and respect. The project identity **Amanah-e-Kisa** is a tribute to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and his blessed family / Ahl al-Kisa / Panjtan, while the public app name **Amanah Quran** remains universal and inclusive for all Muslims.

## Spiritual and Brand Foundation

The project is inspired by:

1. Love and service of the Holy Quran.
2. Reverence for the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and his Ahl al-Bayt.
3. The concept of Amanah: preserving and presenting Quranic content with utmost care.
4. The idea that access to Quran should not be commercialized through ads, tracking, or paid barriers.

Suggested mission line:

> A verified, offline, ad-free Quran app built as an Amanah.

Suggested longer mission line:

> Amanah Quran is a free, offline, ad-free Quran app developed as a sacred trust, inspired by love for the Quran and reverence for the family of the Prophet ﷺ.

## Product Thesis

The market already has many Quran apps, but the opportunity is to build a Quran app that is:

* Completely free.
* Completely ad-free.
* Offline-first.
* Privacy-first.
* Verified and transparent.
* IndoPak-friendly.
* Urdu-friendly.
* Hifz-friendly.
* Elder-friendly.
* Simple enough for daily recitation.
* Rich enough for serious study.
* Built as service, not business.

This app should not try to win by adding random features. It should win by trust, polish, simplicity, accessibility, offline reliability, and respect.

## Target Users

Primary users:

1. Muslims in Pakistan who prefer IndoPak script.
2. Urdu-speaking users globally.
3. Huffaz and students doing memorization/revision.
4. Elders who need large text and simple navigation.
5. Daily Quran readers who want no ads and no distractions.
6. Parents/teachers who want a clean Quran app for children.
7. Users who distrust commercial Islamic apps due to ads, tracking, or paid features.

Secondary users:

1. Global Muslims who prefer Uthmani script.
2. Learners who need translation, audio, repeat, and bookmarks.
3. Masjid users who need silent, distraction-free reading.

## Core Competitor Insight

The Quran app market is saturated but not fully solved.

Major competitors identified:

1. Greentech Al Quran — very feature-rich, strong all-rounder.
2. Quran for Android / Quran.com — trusted, open-source, no ads, very popular.
3. Ayah — beautiful, polished, distraction-free reading.
4. Tarteel — strong AI/memorization product, but partly premium/cloud-dependent.
5. Muslim Pro — huge reach, but commercial, ads, privacy concerns.
6. Quran Majeed-type apps — rich but often criticized for ads and interruptions.
7. Simple open-source Quran apps — clean but often limited.

Key market gaps:

1. Ads in religious apps are deeply disliked.
2. Privacy and tracking concerns create trust problems.
3. IndoPak script is often missing or secondary.
4. Urdu/Pakistan-focused experience is often not polished enough.
5. Rich apps can feel cluttered.
6. Hifz tools are often weak, paid, or cloud-dependent.
7. Elder accessibility is under-served.
8. Offline-first design is often incomplete.
9. Users want trust, authenticity, and no monetization friction.

## Must-Have Principles

### Content Principles

1. Never modify Quran text.
2. Display text must remain exactly as verified source text.
3. Search-normalized text must be stored separately from display text.
4. Every Quranic text source must have source name, version, checksum, and attribution.
5. Translation, tafsir, audio, fonts, and layout data must each be license-reviewed separately.
6. Any public release should include scholar/content review before launch.
7. App should include a Trust Center with source transparency.

### Product Principles

1. No ads.
2. No in-app purchases.
3. No donation popups during recitation.
4. No forced login.
5. No unnecessary notifications.
6. No social-media-style distractions.
7. No unnecessary permissions.
8. No analytics unless explicitly optional and privacy-preserving.
9. Quran reading should open instantly.
10. The app should work gracefully on low-end Android phones.

### Technical Principles

1. Offline-first architecture.
2. SQLite-based local Quran database.
3. Optional downloadable content packs.
4. Small initial APK where possible.
5. Fast startup.
6. Reliable Arabic rendering.
7. Tested on common Android brands in Pakistan: Vivo, Oppo, Samsung, Xiaomi, Infinix, Tecno.
8. Local backup/export for bookmarks, notes, and hifz progress.
9. Future cloud sync should be optional, not required.

## Recommended Data Sources

Potential sources to study and license-review:

1. Tanzil Quran text for verified Arabic Quran text.

## Suggested Architecture

### Android stack

* Native Android app.
* Kotlin preferred.
* Jetpack Compose for UI if suitable.
* Room SQLite for local data.
* Media3/ExoPlayer for audio.
* WorkManager for optional downloads.
* DataStore for settings.
* No analytics SDK initially.
* No ad SDK ever.

### Data model

Core tables:

* surahs
* ayahs
* quran_text_uthmani
* quran_text_indopak
* translations
* tafsir
* pages
* juz
* hizb
* ruku
* sajdah
* bookmarks
* notes
* collections
* hifz_status
* revision_schedule
* audio_files
* content_sources
* checksums

### Content verification

Each content pack should include:

* source name
* source URL
* license
* version
* checksum
* import date
* ayah count
* surah count
* validation status

## Current Final Decision

Proceed with the idea.

Reason: Even though the Quran app market is saturated, the charitable impact is high because there remains a meaningful gap for a fully free, ad-free, privacy-first, offline, IndoPak/Urdu-friendly, hifz-friendly, elder-friendly, trust-centered Quran app.

Financial gain is not the goal. The value is accessibility, trust, sadaqah jariyah, and serving users who dislike commercialized religious apps.
